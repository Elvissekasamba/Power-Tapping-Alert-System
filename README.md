# Power-Tapping-Alert-System
System that uses current sensors along the powerline to pin point between 2 locations of A and B where an illegal load has been connected

## Problem Statement
Electricity theft and tempering of the power lines are the major problems that has been faced in the 
power distributing companies for example UEDCL in Uganda and UMEME was heavily facing the same 
problem before handing over to UEDCL. 
And this problem has resulted in several other multiple challenges i.e. 
It has caused a substantial income losses to the distributing companies whereby in Uganda 
UEDCL specified that the country loses over shs 20 billion annually due to illegal connections to 
the power lines. 
- It has also resulted into overloads for example to the transformers and thus leading to voltage 
fluctuations hence damaging electrical infrastructures. 
- It has increased frequent repair and maintenance, which has led to increased operational costs 
in form paying the technicians and engineers, buying equipment like wires. 
- It has also led to increased cases of fire outbreaks and electrocutions that have led to loss of lives or 
severe injuries and property losses (due to fires) 
- Delays in the information received by the authorities due to poor detection techniques that has 
been relaying heavily for example on manual inspections and on consumer complaints. 

This therefore  calls a need for  a real time automated system for detecting tempering in the power lines 
, showing  the accurate  or exact  location where the tempering have occurred and also display the 
magnitude of the power lost due to tempering.  

## Prototype of the System
The prototype of our system used 2 percolators to act as the illegal loads at positions A ans B along the power line.
When they were turned on, the current sensors connected in positions A and B where able to detect that illeagal power was being used along those positions that where displayed on a LCD screen with the GSM sending an alert notification to the admin
